The importance of hand care

  • By Glamour
  • Nov 2020

The word manicure comes from the French (after all the French are famous for the importance they give to beauty) "manucure" which in turn is based on the Latin "manus", hands and care. Hand care was born in India in the Bronze Age, where women, but often also men, used to use natural substances (first of all henna) to color the surface of the nails and distinguish themselves from others. This habit spreads quickly all over the world and is increasingly used as a symbol of power and high social class; examples of women famous for their lacquered nails are Nefertiti, known for her ruby ​​red nail polish, and Cleopatra, who used to wear a light crimson color!

Even the decoration of the nails, done today with nail art that uses rhinestones, glitter and stones, is an ancient practice. Chinese royalty used precious stones and other trinkets that they applied to their nails to distinguish themselves from the common people. Today the multitude of techniques, colors, ornaments that can be used in nail art is so wide that it allows anyone to find the right way to express their personality and energy even through this part of the body!

In addition to the aesthetic question, the nails are also the mirror of our vitality and provide us with precious indications for the evaluation of our state of health and energy, so much so that in traditional Indian and Chinese medicine their careful and accurate observation is a very important diagnostic tool. .

Dry manicure course

  • Course
  • Duration: 4h
  • Max participants: 4/6 people

The DRY MANICURE course is aimed at teaching the student the brand new “Dry Manicure” technique to clean and prepare natural nails in a precise and immediate way. Compared to the classic manicure, the dry manicure allows to avoid the absorption of water in the nails, making them immediately ready for the application of nail polish, semi-permanent, gel or acrylic reconstruction, reinforcement.

With the help not only of classic manicure tools including timeless cuticle pusher, files or scissors, Russian manicure has, as ace up its sleeve, cutter tips. Used to free the nail plate from the cuticles, in this way they allow the nail to gain a few millimeters, giving its part located around the cuticles a regular and clean appearance. The course will allow you to learn how to carry out the various steps of the dry manicure, using different tips of the cutter with delicacy and precision, in order to preserve the integrity of the skin and nails, as well as to obtain a cleaner and longer lasting job.

Course aimed at all those who want to perform a perfect manicure and in particular those who reconstruct nails in gel and acrylic and who apply gel or semi-permanent polish on the natural nail.

Course contents:

  • • Anatomy of the nail plate;
  • • Micromotor and relative tips (cutters);
  • • Demonstration of dry manicure;
  • • Student practice on dry manicure;
  • • Demonstration of the semi-permanent;
  • • Student practice on semi-permanent.

Semipermanent and minigel course

  • Course
  • Duration: 5h
  • Max participants: 4/6 people

The Semi-Permanent Nail Polish and Minigel Course teaches one of the most popular manicures currently, thanks to the use of products which, among their characteristics, have that of speeding up nail coverage. The course does not require previous preparation, it is open to all.

Thanks to the Semi-Permanent Nail Polish it is possible to cover the nails in about 30 minutes, with a subtle and natural result. For this course we use an innovative product that allows us to do even a mini stretch. This treatment is ideal for those who do not want to over-lengthen the nails but only to obtain a well-groomed and natural effect. It guarantees a duration of at least double that of a normal nail polish, facing a much lower expense than that of nail reconstruction.

Course contents:

  • • Explanation and chemistry of Gel Nail Polish products;
  • • Theory on the anatomy of the nail;
  • • Disinfection and sterilization;
  • • The right polymerization;
  • • Difference between LED lamp and UV lamp;
  • • Manicure and preparation of the nail bed;
  • • Demonstration and practice;
  • • Semi-permanent one-color hands;
  • • Semi-permanent hands with french;
  • • Correct removal;
  • • Practice with minigel;
  • • Analysis and discussion of any problems encountered in practice.
