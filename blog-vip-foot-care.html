    What Is A Pedicure And Why Is It Important?

    • By Glamour
    • 2022

    The foot is the basis of our body: it is the support, on which many aspects of our well-being depend. After the heart, it is the second main circulatory pump and biodynamic structure, responsible for posture and the kinetic chain. Furthermore, it is an extraordinary reflexogenic site: under the sole of the foot there are points that correspond to all the internal organs. This is why the foot, for millennia, has been placed in the foreground by all cultures, be they Eastern or Western. So let's reflect on the importance of taking care of our feet.

    Let's see the differences between AESTHETIC PEDICURE and CURATIVE PEDICURE ...

    AESTHETIC PEDICURE is a treatment to be carried out periodically that allows obtaining lasting results. Imperfections such as rough skin, calluses, thickening are eliminated, nails treated and embellished with the most fashionable enamels.

    Usually the first steps of a quality aesthetic pedicure are the following: washing, cleaning, drying and disinfection. At this point the nails can be filed and you can proceed with a foot bath to remove any calluses. At this point you can concentrate on the cuticles and exfoliate with a special cream. Finally, a relaxing massage is performed to reduce tension and improve circulation and muscle tone.

    Instead, we talk about CURATIVE PEDICURE when the feet have pathologies such as fungal infections, thickenings or deep calluses.

    Clearly, to avoid certain pathologies it is advisable to carry out a periodic pedicure, according to the needs of your feet.
