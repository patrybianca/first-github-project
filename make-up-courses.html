Make-up courses

History, importance and meaning

  • By Glamour
  • Nov 2020

Anyone who claims that make-up is a superficial and unimportant practice is very wrong. In fact, it has been present since ancient times and has always been considered a distinctive trait not only of women, but of a society in general. The use of make-up is attested even to prehistoric times, as evidenced by graffiti in which parts of the body designed ad hoc can be seen. According to the drawings, in fact, it was possible to deduce the tribe to which the subject belonged.

Throughout history there have been many style diktats, but the advent of cinema and Hollywood stars has changed (almost) everything. The models of previous eras, the royals and goddesses of the paintings, in the 1900s give way to divas, who become true icons of beauty.Marilyn Monroe make-up artist Allan Snyder, aka Whitey, was the forerunner of contouring as it is today. In fact, he blended some darker foundation on some areas of the diva's face to optically wear it out. "Promise me you'll put makeup on when I die!" are Marilyn's words to her makeup artist.

The advent of the Internet has brought about a mixture of styles and cultures that offers a very broad combination of idols and models of female beauty. Nowadays it is possible, in fact, to choose whether to adopt an "American" make-up if you prefer it rather marked, or "Italian" when you prefer a make-up / no make-up, "French" with red lips and eyeliner ...

The Glamor Make-Up Courses, the result of more than 20 years of training activities, are training courses that offer solid and complete professional training, thanks to the level of specificity and completeness of the programs and the support of a consolidated teaching methodology. based on the Certified Method. The course programs are preparatory to specializations that open the doors to the world of fashion and entertainment.

Who are they for?

The courses are aimed at those who have a great passion for make-up and who want to grow professionally by putting their talent into play and learning the profession of the Make-Up Artist.

It is not necessary to have a cultural background in the world of beauty and make-up, because even those who start from scratch manage to conclude the academic path with great results.At the end of the courses, certificates will be delivered demonstrating the skills acquired during the Glamour courses.

Cosmetics is the science of the woman's cosmos.

Karl Kraus

Basic make-up course

  • Course
  • Duration: 2 days
  • Max participants: 4 people

This course is designed for those who would like to undertake the profession of make-up artist, but also for all those people who want to understand more about make-up, for example if you work in a perfumery, or in a make-up shop or even if you are a beauty consultant but you are not able to recommend the right foundation to your clients, this course will give you the opportunity to deepen even the theory of colors and to be able to create tricks that improve the person.



Course contents:

  • • History of fashion of the eyebrow arch;
  • • Study of the face, eye, cheekbone and lips;
  • • Theory of color;
  • • The choice of foundation and concealer;
  • • The powder;
  • • chiaroscuro technique;
  • • The perfect base for all skin types and needs;
  • • Eye makeup and its technique;
  • • Natural make-up.

Make-up contouring course

  • Course
  • Duration: 2 days
  • Max participants: X people

Contouring is a make-up technique that gives definition and three-dimensionality to the face based on the fact of creating areas and plays of chiaroscuro in certain points of the face. The Beauty Contouring Make-Up course is aimed at all those who are passionate about make-up and who intend to acquire in-depth preparation on contouring to create day, evening and bridal makeup.

It is possible to access the course even if you do not have any type of experience in this field.

Course contents:

  • • History of make-up - Introduction to cosmetic products;
  • • Application and use of the products;
  • • Epithelial correctors;
  • • Liquid, semi-liquid, creamy, compact, powder foundation;
  • • Light concealer, dark concealer;
  • • Face powders;
  • • Accessories: brushes, latex / synthetic / natural sponges, eyebrow pliers, eyelash curler, palette;
  • • Face corrections: square, triangular, round, long, trapezoidal, oval Corrections of the forehead, temples;
  • • Eyebrow corrections - round, bulging, drooping, sunken, narrow eyes, dark circles, bags;
  • • Corrections aquiline, wide, flat, long, deviated nose;
  • • Lip corrections - cheekbone, receding chin, pronounced, double chin;
  • • Neck and décolleté corrections;
  • • Corrective makeup.
